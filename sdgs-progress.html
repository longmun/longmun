Progress Report 2024 - Working Towards 2030

About the SDGs

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a universal call to action to end poverty, protect the planet, and ensure that all people enjoy peace and prosperity. Adopted by all UN Member States in 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda, the 17 SDGs form an integrated and indivisible framework for achieving sustainable development.

The goals address global challenges including poverty, inequality, climate change, environmental degradation, peace, and justice. Progress has been made in various areas, but accelerated action is needed to achieve the 2030 targets.

Key Challenges

  • Financing Gap: Annual shortfall of $2-3 trillion in developing countries
  • COVID-19 Impact: Pandemic reversed progress on poverty, health, and education
  • Climate Crisis: Global warming accelerating faster than mitigation efforts
  • Geopolitical Tensions: Conflicts diverting resources from development
  • Inflation & Economic Slowdown: Reducing spending on social programs
  • Digital Divide: Technology access disparities between regions
  • Biodiversity Loss: Extinction rates exceeding natural recovery
  • Data Gaps: Insufficient monitoring in least developed countries
